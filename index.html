Course Reflection

01

Introduction

Taking GED0001 has been an enlightening and transformative educational experience. Initially, I expected a standard reading and writing class, but the course quickly challenged me to analyze texts on a much deeper level, particularly regarding the intersection of modern technology and learning.

02

Body 1: Critical Analysis of AI

One of the most valuable aspects of the course was exploring the impact of generative AI and LLMs on education. Through analyzing research like Tossell's study on ChatGPT, I realized that AI isn't a magic shortcut. Instead, it alters the cognitive load of a task, often making critical thought and synthesis even more complex than before.

03

Body 2: The Digital Deluge

My perspective on daily technology use shifted drastically when reading about the "Digital Deluge" and the hippocampus. Learning that simple conveniences like GPS actually deactivate parts of the brain responsible for cognitive mapping forced me to re-evaluate my own dependence on technology and how passively I consume information.

04

Body 3: Evolving Writing Skills

Writing regular responses and completing reading process worksheets significantly sharpened my analytical skills. Deconstructing arguments to find the author's true intent, recognizing biases, and evaluating evidence are skills I had to actively practice and refine over the semester.

05

Conclusion

Ultimately, GED0001 has equipped me with more than just basic reading comprehension. It has taught me to navigate a knowledge economy critically—to question the tools I use, preserve my cognitive engagement, and articulate my thoughts logically. These lessons will remain invaluable throughout my academic and professional career.
